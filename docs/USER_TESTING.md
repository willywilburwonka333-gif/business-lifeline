# Business Lifeline User Testing Pack

## Purpose

Use this with real small-business owners, accountants, advisers, or people who have managed business pressure. Do not coach them through the product unless they become completely stuck.

## Test setup

Ask the tester to use either:

- the Riverbend Café demo, or
- their own approximate business figures if they are comfortable doing so

Tell them this is a prototype and not professional advice.

## Core tasks

1. Open the product and explain what it does in their own words.
2. Open the Riverbend Café demo.
3. Identify the business’s most urgent issue.
4. Find the three immediate priorities.
5. Find the recommended recovery playbook.
6. Ask Business Brain one genuine decision question.
7. Use the Cashflow Simulator to improve the monthly result.
8. Explain the difference between monthly improvement and one-off cash.
9. Find the professional-escalation guidance.
10. Find a practical template in Resources.

## Questions to ask after testing

### Understanding

- What did you think the product was for?
- Did you understand the health score?
- Did the top priorities feel clear?
- Could you tell which information was calculated and which was AI interpretation?

### Usefulness

- Which feature felt most useful?
- Did the simulator change how you thought about the business problem?
- Would this help you prepare for a meeting with an accountant or adviser?
- Would you return weekly during a recovery?

### Trust

- Did anything sound too certain?
- Did the professional-help warnings feel appropriate?
- Did you understand that this was decision support rather than professional advice?
- What would you need before entering real business information?

### Friction

- Where did you hesitate?
- Which words or figures were confusing?
- Was anything too long or crowded?
- Did any button fail or lead somewhere unexpected?
- What should be removed rather than added?

## Simple rating sheet

Ask for a 1–10 score for:

- clarity
- usefulness
- trust
- visual quality
- ease of navigation
- likelihood of using it
